# Workflow: Pre-Call Research Brief

Walk into every call already knowing who you're talking to and what they probably want. This workflow builds the briefing you never have time to build manually.

## What it does

You give it a prospect and their company. It produces a one-page brief:

1. Who this person is and what they're likely measured on
2. What the company is doing right now that's relevant to you
3. Three plausible reasons they took the call
4. The smartest questions to ask, tailored to them
5. One credibility-earning insight to open with

## The instruction set

```
You are my pre-call research assistant. Build me a briefing for an upcoming call.

WHAT I'LL GIVE YOU:
- The prospect's name, title, and company
- Their LinkedIn text and/or the company website content (I'll paste it)
- How the call got booked (inbound, cold, referral, etc.)
- What I sell, in one sentence

BUILD THE BRIEF:
1. THE PERSON — what they likely own and are measured on; what keeps them up at night in that role
2. THE COMPANY — recent, relevant signals (funding, hiring, launches, news) and what each implies for me
3. WHY THIS CALL — three ranked hypotheses for why they're talking to me
4. QUESTIONS — the 5 discovery questions most likely to surface real pain for THIS person; mark the top 3 if I only get 15 minutes
5. OPENER — one specific, non-obvious observation I can lead with to earn credibility fast
6. WATCH-OUTS — anything I should NOT say, and any red flag to check for

Label anything that's a guess as a guess. Keep it to one page.
```

## Connected version

If your AI can search the web or reach your CRM and email history:

```
CONNECTED ACTIONS:
- Pull our past interactions with this company from CRM and email, and summarize
  the relationship history at the top of the brief.
- Search for recent news about the company (last 3 months) and include only what's
  relevant to my product.
- Note the last thing we promised them, if anything, so I don't drop it.
```

## Why this beats "I'll just wing it"

Prospects can tell in the first two minutes whether you did your homework. A specific opener ("saw you just moved the whole team onto X") buys you credibility that a generic "so tell me about your challenges" never will. This workflow makes that preparation take two minutes instead of twenty, which is the difference between doing it every time and doing it never.

## Field notes

- **The opener is the payoff.** If the brief gives you one genuinely specific thing to say in minute one, it did its job.
- **Feed it real source material.** The brief is only as good as what you paste in. Grab the LinkedIn "About" and recent posts, plus the company's homepage and any news.
- **Pair it with the discovery framework.** The brief gets you in the door; the [discovery structure](https://github.com/elizavetasukhonosova/sales-for-founders/blob/main/templates/discovery-call-framework.md) runs the actual call.
- **Don't read the brief on the call.** Absorb it beforehand. Reading notes makes you sound like you're reading notes.
